Season 4, 2025

2025-12-22

In a year of huge seasons, season 4 did not relent. Kicking off with a big trip to Melbourne and Sydney and continuing to be pretty jam packed all the way up to the end is honestly just par for the course for 2025.

Goals from this season

> Make a new friend at MIGW / SXSW
Mission accomplished! Met a few nice people in the southern states from all over the country (and the world, really!) Writing this now is reminding me I should probably reach out to them and let them know it was nice to meed them hehe. I already did a whole write-up about MIGW & SXSW 2025, so give it a read if you're interested.

> Cancel on things (or at least be late)
Writing this goal down did give me license to put myself first a bit more and be in tune with my own needs. There was only one instance I can remember where I actually cancelled on something because I wasn't feeling it, but I was able to loosen up on punctuality pressure for both myself and others which I think is good.

> Dedicate a few hours for chilling each week
I've written this as a goal before, and I've never followed through (tbh, it's silly to have a goal that is "plan something" like, just make the plan now, buster). I think it's a reflex for feeling overwhelmed and out of control. I took some steps to prioritise sleep and some recharge time this season and I think I've reached an understanding of my energy levels (something I've struggled with all year). Next step for this is being able to anticipate if something is going to send me into an energy deficit. I'll be keeping tabs.

> Gear up for 2026
As everything's wound down, I honestly haven't thought much about 2026. There are a few projects carrying through to the new year, which is reassuring, but largely there isn't anything huge looming on the horizon. I guess this very post is acting as a part of this goal?

> Develop a music practise & composition routine
I did manage to sit at the piano a lot more than is usual this season. Counterintuitively, this wasn't due to effective goal setting or carefully laid out plans, but instead, a commitment to just being at the piano. Czerny has been a mainstay, and has helped my technique in a pretty short amount of time. I've also been grinding some Bach and Debussy to keep things challenging.
I'm not going to be performing and recitals any time soon, but this helps me feel more adept, and less floundering when I need my fingers to be ready to play something. I'm hoping I can focus my piano time a little more in the new year when I can be more intentioned with my time and schedule. Really getting deep into some jazz stuff will be the next road to go down I think.

In terms of overall recap, MIGW and SXSW really took the limelight this season, and as I said earlier, they got their own recap. As I had anticipated, October was pretty much a write off, as was December with mostly residency wind down stuff and the holidays in the last two weeks. November didn't really have anything of note going on, so that pretty much concludes the recap haha! As you can see below I did end up watching and playing a bunch of stuff in my trip recovery week and when I had the flu for a week (lame). Totally fine, just not very exiting to write about here lol.

Forecast: The whole of 2026

The year's end is a perfect time to look forward a bit further than just one season, so let's scope out 2026 as a whole. There are lots of things I would like to do in the new year, and I definitely won't be able to do them all at once. Here's some I can think of right now:

  • Make music incessantly
    I want to improve my proficiency and confidence with making music. Part of this comes down to making time for myself, and also stying diciplined. I'm confident I can tackle this in 2026, and I'm hoping to even share some of the stuff on this here website.
  • Stream more
    I enjoy live streaming, and I think it could be beneficial for me in many ways. I want to establish a consistent schedule which includes both working and games streams.
  • More SPU!
    I've said it before and I'll say it again: SPU was a great experience and I want to work with those fantastic musicians again! Looking to perform more Crash shows and hopefully add another game to the repertoire.
  • Try to stay afloat through the whole year
    I'm in the fortunate position of being self employed right now, but I know that it's not going to be easy to keep it that way. Tough decisions will need to be made, but I'm hopeful that I can keep it rolling for the rest of the year.
  • Become a house plant guy
    This is a small one, but our place is in serious need of some greenery, and we haven't been able to sustain plants since we moved in. So now is the time to learn the ins and outs of plant care.
  • Publish more games
    I really enjoy working on small projects that can be released before you have a chance to second guess them. Already got one jam lined up for January, and I'll be keeping my eye out for more throughout the year.
  • Do Games Week again
    I've enjoyed MIGW every time I've gone, and I'd like to go again. This year, the financials are going to be particularly restrictive, which means I'll have to look for grants or tickets through giving talks or smth.
  • Start doing local audio meetups
    Since covid, the local games audio scene has become more scattered, and I'm hoping to bring it back. It's not going to be easy, but I think it will strengthen the local scene a lot. I'm planning on starting super small, but I'm defs hoping it will grow to something more self-sustaining.
  • Work more publicly
    Again, since I'm going to be a lot more isolated this year, it will be important that I share my work (expecially online). I've struggled with this before, but making it a core part of the process will ideally make it easier.
  • Keep tinkering away at this website (and memorycardaudio.com)
